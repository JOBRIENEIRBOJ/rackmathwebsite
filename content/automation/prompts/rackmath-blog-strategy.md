# RackMath Blog Strategy

## Blog Purpose

The RackMath blog helps beginners and returning lifters get comfortable with resistance training.

The blog should make weight lifting feel:

- Less confusing
- Less intimidating
- More practical
- More repeatable
- More approachable

The goal is not to impress advanced lifters.

The goal is to help the person who just got a gym membership, walked into the weight room, and immediately thought:

> "I have no idea what I am doing."

## Core Promise

RackMath helps lifters spend less time guessing and more time training.

The blog should support that promise by teaching simple, useful lessons about:

- Starting weight lifting
- Learning basic gym equipment
- Loading barbells
- Tracking workouts
- Building confidence
- Improving form
- Progressing safely
- Staying consistent

## Primary Audience

The primary reader is:

- New to lifting
- Returning after a long break
- Nervous in the gym
- Confused by racks, plates, bars, sets, reps, and progression
- Interested in health, strength, and long-term fitness
- Not trying to become a bodybuilder overnight
- Looking for practical answers

## Secondary Audience

The secondary reader may be:

- A busy adult trying to improve health
- A parent trying to stay strong
- A former athlete returning to training
- A coach helping beginners
- Someone who wants a simple weight lifting calculator
- Someone looking for a barbell plate calculator

## Article Format

Most articles should follow this structure:

1. Hook
2. The simple truth
3. Why this matters
4. What beginners usually get wrong
5. What to do instead
6. How RackMath helps
7. Final thought
8. Sources

## Ideal Article Length

Most articles should be:

- 700–1,100 words for standard blog posts
- 300–600 words for short helpful posts
- 1,200–1,800 words only for major pillar articles

Avoid making articles long just to make them long.

Primary keyword themes:

- weight lifting calculator
- barbell plate calculator
- plate calculator
- gym plate calculator
- workout tracker
- beginner weight lifting
- how to start lifting weights
- how to load a barbell
- what weight should I start lifting
- how many days a week should beginners lift

## Internal Linking Strategy

Every article should naturally link to relevant RackMath pages when possible.

Use internal links for:

- Plate calculator
- Workout tracker
- Blog archive
- Beginner guides
- Related articles

Internal links should feel helpful, not forced.

Good example:

> If plate math keeps slowing you down, use RackMath to load the bar correctly and move on with your workout.

Bad example:

> Click here now to use the best calculator ever made.

## Conversion Strategy

The blog should not hard-sell.

Instead, it should create moments where the reader thinks:

> "That problem annoys me. RackMath would make that easier."

Good RackMath tie-ins:

- "RackMath helps you calculate plates fast."
- "RackMath helps you track your sets so you are not guessing next week."
- "RackMath helps reduce one small piece of gym confusion."

## Trust Strategy

Use citations for health and training claims.

Cite:

- CDC
- WHO
- ACSM
- NIH / PubMed
- Mayo Clinic
- Cleveland Clinic
- Other reputable medical, health, or academic sources

Do not invent citations.

Do not overstate claims.
