# RackMath Editing and Proofreading Prompt

Use this prompt after drafting a RackMath article.

```txt
Edit this RackMath article.

Goals:
- Make it clearer.
- Make it shorter where needed.
- Keep the voice real and beginner-friendly.
- Cut fluff.
- Remove unnecessary adjectives.
- Remove repeated ideas.
- Keep paragraphs short.
- Make it readable by a 12-year-old.
- Respect adult readers.
- Keep practical advice specific.
- Check that every health, science, or training claim has a citation.
- Make sure the RackMath mention is useful, not salesy.
- Return the final article in Markdown with frontmatter.

RackMath voice:
- Plainspoken
- Real
- Practical
- Slightly vulnerable when appropriate
- Occasionally funny
- No hype
- No bro-science
- No long academic explanations

Editing priorities:
1. Clarity
2. Usefulness
3. Simplicity
4. Trust
5. Reader confidence

Before returning the final version, silently check:
- Is the opening engaging?
- Does the article answer one clear question?
- Are the steps practical?
- Is anything too wordy?
- Are any claims unsupported?
- Does the ending give the reader a clear takeaway?
```

## Line-Level Editing Checklist

Check each paragraph.

Ask:

- Can this be shorter?
- Is this sentence doing real work?
- Is this advice specific?
- Would a beginner understand this?
- Is this tone natural?
- Is there a simpler word?
- Did we use too many adjectives?
- Did we accidentally sound like a fitness influencer?

## Words and Phrases to Cut

Usually cut or replace:

- ultimate
- revolutionary
- transformative
- game-changing
- unlock
- dominate
- crush
- unleash
- optimize, unless truly needed
- maximize, unless truly needed
- embark on your journey
- achieve your goals
- fitness journey
- take your training to the next level

## Better Replacements

Instead of:

> Optimize your training experience.

Use:

> Make your workouts easier to follow.

Instead of:

> Transform your body with resistance training.

Use:

> Strength training can help you build muscle, get stronger, and move better over time.

Instead of:

> Crush your workout.

Use:

> Do the work you planned to do.

## Proofreading Checklist

Before publishing:

- Title is clear.
- Description is under control and search-friendly.
- Slug is lowercase and hyphenated.
- Frontmatter is valid.
- Headings are clear.
- Paragraphs are short.
- No obvious typos.
- No broken Markdown.
- No fake citations.
- Sources are listed.
- Internal links are suggested or included.
