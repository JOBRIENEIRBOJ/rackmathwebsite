# Your First Week of Weight Lifting: Exactly What to Do

Walking into the gym for the first time can feel like walking into a foreign country.

Machines everywhere. Barbells. Dumbbells. People who look like they know what they are doing.

You just want to know one thing:

**What exactly should I do in my first week of weight lifting?**

This guide will give you a simple, repeatable plan.

No fancy exercises. No “grind harder” speeches. Just clear steps.

---

## The simple truth

Your first week of lifting does not need to be impressive.

It needs to be:

- Short
- Simple
- Safe
- Repeatable

The goal is not to “go all out.”

The goal is to **learn a few basic movements, use light weights, and leave the gym feeling like you could come back tomorrow.**

Major health organizations recommend doing muscle-strengthening activities at least two days per week.[^1][^2]

If you can get through **three short beginner workouts** this week, you are already ahead of that.

---

## Why this matters

Strength training can help you build and maintain muscle, support joint and bone health, and make everyday tasks like carrying groceries or climbing stairs easier.[^3][^4]

It is also one part of a healthy activity plan recommended for adults.[^1][^2][^3]

But here is the catch:

Most beginners never make it past the first week because:

- They do way too much.
- They lift way too heavy.
- They get so sore or stressed that they never want to go back.

A calmer first week fixes that.

You will still work hard, but in a way your body and brain can handle.

---

## The plan for your first week of weight lifting

You will do **3 beginner gym workouts** this week.

- **Day 1:** Full body A  
- **Day 2:** Rest or light movement (walk, easy bike, etc.)
- **Day 3:** Full body B  
- **Day 4:** Rest or light movement  
- **Day 5:** Full body A again  
- **Day 6–7:** Rest or light movement

You will lift **3 days**, not 6.

That is enough for beginners and matches the idea of doing muscle-strengthening work at least twice per week.[^1][^2]

Each workout will hit:

- Legs
- Push (chest/shoulders/triceps)
- Pull (back/biceps)
- Core

Each session should take about **30–45 minutes**, including rests.

---

## How to pick your starting weight (simple rule)

For this first week, use this rule:

> **Pick a weight you can lift for the target reps, with control, while still being able to stop 2–3 reps before “absolute max.”**

If you are not sure, **start lighter than you think**.

You can always add weight next week.

If you feel any sharp pain, numbness, or something that feels “wrong” (not just effort or normal muscle burn), stop that exercise and choose an easier variation or ask staff for help.

---

## Workout A (Day 1 and Day 5)

Warm up first:

- 5 minutes easy cardio (walk, bike, or treadmill)
- Then 1 very light warm-up set for the first two exercises

**Exercise 1 – Leg Press (or Goblet Squat)**  
- 3 sets of 8–10 reps  
- Rest 60–90 seconds between sets  
- Start with a weight that feels “easy-medium” on the first set

**Exercise 2 – Seated Chest Press Machine (or Push-Ups on an incline)**  
- 3 sets of 8–10 reps  
- Rest 60–90 seconds

**Exercise 3 – Seated Row Machine**  
- 3 sets of 8–10 reps  
- Rest 60–90 seconds

**Exercise 4 – Dumbbell Shoulder Press (seated, if possible)**  
- 2 sets of 10–12 reps  
- Rest 60–90 seconds  
- Use light dumbbells you can control the whole time

**Exercise 5 – Plank (or elevated plank on bench)**  
- 2–3 sets of 15–30 seconds  
- Rest 45–60 seconds

### How this should feel

- Last 2–3 reps of each set feel **challenging but not ugly**.
- You can still breathe and control the movement.
- You should not be shaking or failing on the first week.

If you finish all sets and feel like you could easily do **5 or more extra reps**, make a note to **add a small amount of weight next week**, not right away.

---

## Workout B (Day 3)

Warm up:

- 5 minutes easy cardio
- 1 light warm-up set for the first two exercises

**Exercise 1 – Dumbbell Romanian Deadlift (RDL)**  
- 3 sets of 8–10 reps  
- Rest 60–90 seconds  
- Keep your back neutral, hinge at the hips (like closing a car door with your butt), dumbbells near your legs

**Exercise 2 – Lat Pulldown Machine**  
- 3 sets of 8–10 reps  
- Rest 60–90 seconds

**Exercise 3 – Dumbbell Bench Press (or machine chest press again)**  
- 3 sets of 8–10 reps  
- Rest 60–90 seconds

**Exercise 4 – Machine or Cable Row (any row variation if seated row is taken)**  
- 2–3 sets of 8–10 reps  
- Rest 60–90 seconds

**Exercise 5 – Simple Core (choose one)**  
Pick one of:
- Dead bug (lying on back, moving opposite arm/leg slowly)
- Bird dog (on hands/knees, reaching opposite arm/leg)

Do:
- 2 sets of 8–10 slow reps per side  
- Rest 45–60 seconds

### How this should feel

Similar to Workout A:

- You are working, but you are not destroyed.
- Form stays controlled.
- No racing through reps.

If your form breaks down before you hit the planned reps, **lower the weight** on the next set.

## What beginners usually get wrong in week one

Most new lifters think:

- “More is better.”
- “If I am not crawling out of the gym, it didn’t count.”
- “I should copy the person lifting the heaviest.”

So they:

- Do 10–12 different exercises.
- Turn every set into a max effort.
- Skip rest days.
- Never write anything down.
- Guess the weight each time they go back.

This often leads to:

- Soreness that makes normal life hard.
- Feeling embarrassed or overwhelmed.
- Quitting after a few attempts.

You do not need to “earn” your place in the gym by suffering.

You need a **simple plan you can repeat.**

---

## What to do instead (step-by-step)

Here is your **first week of weight lifting**, written out.

### Before you go to the gym

1. **Decide your three days now.**  
   For example: Monday, Wednesday, Friday.

2. **Write down the Workout A and B exercises** in a notes app or notebook.  
   So you are not standing in the weight room scrolling random workouts.

3. **Plan 45 minutes total per session.**  
   Enough time to move without rushing.

---

### In every workout

Follow this simple checklist:

1. **Warm up for 5 minutes.**  
   Easy pace. You should be able to talk in full sentences.

2. **Use light weights at first.**  
   First set of the first exercise should feel very manageable.

3. **Move with control.**  
   - 2 seconds to lift  
   - 2 seconds to lower  
   No bouncing or throwing weights.

4. **Rest 60–90 seconds between sets.**  
   Use this time to shake out your arms, take a breath, and log what you just did.

5. **Stop sets with 2–3 reps left “in the tank.”**  
   You should feel like you *could* do more if you had to.

6. **Notice how your body feels.**  
   Normal: muscles working, breathing harder, mild burn.  
   Not normal: sharp pain, sudden “pinch,” dizziness. If that happens, stop and adjust.

---

### How to track what you did (so week two is easier)

Tracking is what turns random workouts into **progress**.

You only need to record three things for each exercise:

- Exercise name  
- Weight used  
- Sets x reps

Example from Workout A:

- Leg Press – 70 lb – 3 x 10  
- Chest Press Machine – 40 lb – 3 x 9  
- Seated Row – 55 lb – 3 x 10  
- DB Shoulder Press – 10 lb – 2 x 12  
- Plank – 3 x 20 seconds

That is it.

Next week, you can:

- Add 5–10 lb to a few lifts **or**
- Try to do 1–2 more reps with the same weight

Both are progress.

## What to expect after your first week

You might feel:

- Mildly sore in the muscles you trained, especially 24–48 hours later.
- A bit tired from using muscles you have not used in a while.
- Slightly more confident just from knowing where things are in the gym.

You should **not** feel:

- Pain in joints or sharp pain anywhere.
- So exhausted you cannot manage normal life.
- Like you never want to see a gym again.

If soreness is uncomfortable, light walking or gentle movement can help. This kind of short-term muscle soreness is common when you start new activities.[^3]

If something feels more serious or does not improve, talk with a healthcare professional.

---

## Final thought

Your first week of weight lifting does not need to be perfect.

It needs to be:

- 3 simple workouts  
- Light, controlled weights  
- Basic notes on what you did

Do that, and you are not “trying lifting.”

You are **actually lifting.**

Next week, you will not be starting from zero. You will be building from your notes.
